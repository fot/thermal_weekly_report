Configuration:

In April of 2008 the ISIM detector housing heaters were disabled and are expected to remain in this state for the remainder of the mission.

On April 28th of 2009 the telescope secondary database calibration was changed to reflect the A side calibration with non-critical heater setpoints set to 66F instead of their nominal calibration for the B side heaters. This change allows the secondary database to be used as a contingency option to drain the batteries should Chandra enter a short eclipse too hot. Ensuring the batteries are properly drained prior to exiting a short eclipse will help prevent the power bus from exceeding 35v.

In August of 2009 the ISIM FA6 abort heater was disabled and is expected to remain in this state for the remainder of the mission.

On December 8th, 2014, the telescope primary, secondary, and tertiary databases were modified to switch all thermistors that contribute to the OBA_AVE and TSSMAX telescope healthchecks to wide range read mode to prevent several of these thermistors from reaching a temperature saturation point. This change ensures temperatures in these regions will continue to be fully visible for the forseeable future.
